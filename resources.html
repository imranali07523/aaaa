Stay Aware

  • Always be aware of your surroundings
  • Avoid using headphones in unfamiliar areas
  • Trust your instincts if something feels wrong
  • Keep your phone charged and accessible

Travel Safety

  • Share your travel plans with trusted contacts
  • Use registered and verified transportation
  • Avoid traveling alone late at night
  • Keep emergency contacts on speed dial

Online Safety

  • Don't share personal information with strangers
  • Be cautious about meeting online contacts
  • Use privacy settings on social media
  • Report harassment or threats immediately

Home Safety

  • Keep doors and windows locked
  • Install security cameras if possible
  • Know your neighbors and local police station
  • Don't open doors to strangers

If You Feel Threatened

  1. Move to a well-lit, crowded area immediately
  2. Call emergency services (100 or 112)
  3. Alert people nearby about the situation
  4. Use the SOS feature on this website
  5. Don't confront the person alone

If You're Being Followed

  1. Don't go home - head to a public place
  2. Call someone and speak loudly about where you are
  3. Enter a shop, restaurant, or police station
  4. Change directions multiple times to confirm
  5. Call police immediately if threat continues

If Someone is Harassing You

  1. Firmly say "NO" and ask them to stop
  2. Document the incident (time, place, details)
  3. Report to local police or women's helpline
  4. Seek support from friends, family, or counselors
  5. File a complaint if harassment continues

Self-Defense Basics

  1. Take self-defense classes if possible
  2. Carry pepper spray or whistle (where legal)
  3. Target vulnerable areas: eyes, nose, throat, groin
  4. Make noise - scream, yell, use alarms
  5. Escape is always the priority over fighting
